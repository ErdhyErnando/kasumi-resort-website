Cidahu in Sukabumi sits at the edge of **Mount Halimun Salak National Park (TNGHS)**. The air is cool, roads wind through hills, and there are plenty of outdoor stops that work well for a weekend. Here are five destinations that are popular with both domestic and international travelers — easy to pair with a stay at the resort.

## 1. Halimun Salak National Park (Cidahu side)

This protected area offers tropical rainforest, bird-filled trekking trails, and rangers who help keep the ecosystem healthy. Visitors often come for easy hikes, birdwatching, or simply breathing under the canopy. Always check opening hours, permits, and park rules before you go.

## 2. Waterfalls and streams near Cidahu

Across Sukabumi’s valleys, **waterfalls** are a classic draw: the sound of water, small pools, and a break from city noise. Pick a trail that matches your fitness, wear grippy shoes, and pack light — skip single-use plastics when you can.

## 3. Situ Gunung and the suspension bridge

**Situ Gunung** is known for its calm lake and gentle outdoor activities. The **suspension bridge** is a well-known photo spot for visitors from Indonesia and abroad. Early mornings often bring softer light and fewer crowds.

## 4. Mountain views and golden hour

Sukabumi offers many viewpoints for layered ridges and morning mist. If you enjoy photography or easy walks, planning around **golden hour** can be the most memorable part — without chasing extreme summits.

## 5. Local food and souvenirs

After a day outside, stopping at a small seafood stall or a local coffee shop is a tasty way to meet Sukabumi. Try regional dishes, traditional snacks, and hot drinks — culture completes the nature trip.
